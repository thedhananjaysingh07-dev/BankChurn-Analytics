EUROPEAN BANKING BENCHMARKS
Source: PwC Strategy& Retail Banking Monitor 2025
=======================================================

CHURN BENCHMARKS
-----------------
Industry avg churn rate : 5–15%
Our dataset churn rate  : ~20% (above average — red flag)
Highest churn country   : Germany (32.4%)
#1 churn reason         : Poor customer experience
                          (laggy UX, rigid policies, 
                           broken support, fees with 
                           no service improvement)

2024 MARKET KPIs
-----------------
Deposit volume growth   : +2%
Loan volume growth      : +1%
Topline (Revenue)       : +3%
Operating costs         : +3%  (margins not improving)
Operating profit        : +4%  (efficiency-driven)

INCOME PER CUSTOMER (2024)
---------------------------
Total income/customer   : €658
Net interest income     : €454 (69%) ← dominant driver
Fee & commission income : €178 (27%)
Trend 2018→2024         : €548 → €658 (+20% over 6 years)

KEY ANALYTICAL IMPLICATIONS
-----------------------------
1. Our dataset churn (~20%) exceeds industry avg (15%)
   — signals an above-average retention problem
2. Germany's 32.4% churn confirms H2 hypothesis
3. Rising income/customer means high-balance churners
   cost more than just their balance suggests
4. Cost growing = profits = banks need retention,
   not just acquisition

TERMINOLOGY
-----------
Topline     = Revenue (top line of P&L statement)
Bottom line = Net Profit
